Water Tester — Full Technical Details

This project was built to measure water pollutants in real-time. The design used Arduino and required careful balance between water pressure and sensor dwell time. I designed the casing in SolidWorks, ensuring it could house batteries, wiring, sensors, and an LCD without looking clunky. Limitations included budget, time, and consumer-friendliness. Despite these, we delivered a working prototype that could be further refined for commercial use. Team size: 3.

AI Eye Tracker — Full Technical Details

Built with Python, OpenCV, and custom velocity prediction formulas. Faced major issues with threading limitations, which were resolved using a queuing workaround. We also built a GPIO-based foot pedal (modeled in SolidWorks and 3D printed) to satisfy input constraints. Presented at a showcase where attendees, especially kids, enjoyed interacting with it. Team size: 3.

Magic Mirror — Full Technical Details

This solo project used a Raspberry Pi and a Flutter-based mobile app to communicate and control mirror displays. Cost efficiency was achieved by using an acrylic sheet with two-way mirror film. Key technical challenges included secure communication setup, and future goals include adding voice or touch controls.
