1. Introduction

The Water Quality Tester is a real-time IoT device designed to monitor tap water safety and provide users with immediate insights into contamination levels. Developed as part of a Louisiana Tech University engineering course and competition, this project aimed to address community concerns over water safety, particularly in areas where city filtration systems may fall short.

2. Objectives

  • Measure water quality in real time using accessible sensors.
  • Create a cost-effective, compact, and user-friendly design.
  • Display water safety data on an integrated LCD in understandable terms.
  • Maintain sufficient water flow and pressure for practical use.

3. Design & Planning

The team selected a TDS (Total Dissolved Solids) sensor for its affordability and baseline reliability within project constraints. The mechanical design involved:

  • Integration of the sensor into a water flow housing connected to standard faucets.
  • Accommodating the Arduino microcontroller, wiring, LCD display, and power supply.
  • Designing custom faucet adapters and an enclosure in SolidWorks for 3D printing.

4. Technical Implementation

  • Microcontroller: Arduino Uno to control sensor input and display output.
  • Sensor: TDS sensor to monitor water quality.
  • Display: LCD programmed to show continuous real-time safety indicators.
  • Enclosure: Water-resistant casing designed with pressure management in mind.
  • Software: Custom Arduino script to filter readings, compare them with safety benchmarks, and translate results into clear warnings.

5. Challenges

  • Cost: Staying within a limited student budget while achieving reliable readings.
  • Size & Pressure: Keeping components small and ensuring proper water flow.
  • Data Translation: Turning raw TDS values into actionable safety indicators understandable by everyday users.

6. Testing & Validation

Tested on tap water, filtered water, and artificially contaminated samples. The device accurately detected relative contamination differences and visually warned users when thresholds were exceeded.

7. Future Improvements

  • Incorporating multi-parameter sensors (pH, turbidity, and heavy metals).
  • Further miniaturization and professional-grade water resistance.
  • Long-term durability testing and battery optimization.

8. Reflection

This project pushed us to bridge hardware design and software logic under real-world constraints. We delivered a fully functional, consumer-facing prototype validated by testing. With more resources, I would optimize both hardware and the data display system for commercialization and real consumer use.
